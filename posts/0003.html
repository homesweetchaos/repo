Take a freaking shower!

Have you ever noticed how a shower has the power to revitalize you? It can completely change your mood, calm you down when you’re feeling overwhelmed, or energize you when you need a little pep in your step. It’s so simple, yet

For me, a shower totally chills out my ADHD, anxiety, and anything else that is going on in my brain. Water is healing, physically, emotionally, and spiritually. You can wash away your stresses and anxieties, albeit temporarily, that’s a pretty damn good power!

So why, for the love of all that is holy, why is it so hard to take a freaking shower when I’m stressed, anxious, overwhelmed, depressed, or in a mind-sucking project that my ADHD throws at me? Sometimes I have to tell myself, “TAKE A FREAKING SHOWER!” And the longer I go without one, the less I want one. Is anyone else like this?

Anyway, I had a rough day today. Nothing bad happened, my brain was just up to no good and was thinking and stuff (you know how dangerous that can be), and all those little stresses that have been growing slowly and steadily finally seemed overwhelming. Everything seemed too big and out of my immediate control.

After a little internal struggle I got in the shower and as soon as that water hit me, I felt it all wash away and go down the drain. It didn’t fix or solve any of my problems, they are all still there, but it helped slow me down enough to realize there is nothing I can do at this moment, and I am taking appropriate steps to improve the situation. Putting so much internal agony and negativity toward this isn’t helping anything. It literally does nothing good.

Wash it all off.

Send that negativity down the drain.

So here is a reminder as we near the end of the week. Let go of your stresses and anxieties. No, don’t give up, just let go of that tension. Take a shower at whatever temperature pleases you, wash your hair, and your body, scrub away the dead skin, impurities, dirt, and grime, and let all that shit wash away down the drain. Step out more relaxed and more capable to do what you need to do.

Elevate the experience

Want to elevate your shower experience? Of course, you do! Although not necessary, you can create an even more relaxing, rejuvenating, stress-reducing, spa-like shower experience with a few extras.

Upgrade shower head.

The sky is the limit when it comes to showerheads. I'm not too picky as long as it has a handheld option, but here are a few upgrades that I thought looked amazing. I might just have to upgrade my showerhead too after shopping around.

From filtered water and increased water pressure, to rainfall shower head with handheld option, to full body jets and all of the above. You really can have it all. In fact, it doesn't stop there either. Someday when I'm rich and famous, I'll have a luxurious steam shower with all the jets, rainfall, or whatever. I'll have water pouring out of every surface if I want! Deep sigh... Someday...

Lighting

I love the ambiant flickering lighting that candles bring, and Tuscany candles are my favorite brand. There isn't a single scent I don't like. My favorite for the bathroom for both soothing and energizing is Soothing Eucalyptus.

But if you don't want a real flame in your home, you can opt for faux candles that can be operated with a remote. These are great because you don't have to replace them (as often), and they still give off that flickering lighting like a real candle. The down side, is you'll have to get your Aromatherapy elsewhere.

Or if you want color options and different modes, you can go with an echo glow. It does require an echo device to run it, but I see that as a benefit rather than a drawback because then you also have music for your shower and never have to touch a screen with wet hands. You can also use it as a bathroom nightlight. This is great option if you have kids.

Aromatherapy

Again, the Tuscany candles are my favorite in this category, but I also really like shower bombs.

Music/sounds

I like to have a smart speaker in my bathroom so if I start singing in the shower, but only know that one line, I can just say, "Alexa, play that one song!"

I also have a JBL bluetooth speaker that is waterproof and the loud shower is no match for it's sound quality and volume.
